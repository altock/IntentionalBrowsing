Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 20, 2026

Intentional Browsing is a browser extension that blocks algorithmic feeds on social media platforms. This policy explains what data the extension accesses and how it's used.

Data Collection

This extension does not collect, transmit, or share any personal data. There are no analytics, no tracking, and no external servers.

Data Stored Locally

The following data is stored locally on your device using your browser's built-in storage:

  • Your preferences - which platforms are enabled, redirect destinations, and blocking settings

This data never leaves your browser and is not accessible to anyone but you.

Host Permissions

The extension requires access to specific social media websites (Twitter/X, Reddit, YouTube, Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, TikTok) solely to:

  • Detect when you navigate to algorithmic feed URLs
  • Redirect blocked pages to safe destinations
  • Hide feed elements on pages using DOM manipulation

The extension does not read, store, or transmit any content from these websites.

Third Parties

This extension does not share any data with third parties. There are no external services, APIs, or analytics providers.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes, the updated date at the top will be revised. Significant changes will be noted in the extension's changelog.
